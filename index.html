About ESDEI

The Eastlake Speech & Debate Education Initiative (ESDEI) is a student-led program dedicated to introducing middle school students to the art of public speaking and competitive debate. Founded by members of the Eastlake High School Speech and Debate Club, we believe that every student deserves the opportunity to develop critical thinking skills, build confidence, and find their voice.

Our Mission

Addressing Political Polarization Through Education

In an era of increasing division, ESDEI is tackling one of our nation's most pressing challenges: political polarization. By teaching young people to genuinely understand and articulate multiple perspectives on complex issues, we're building a foundation for a more civically engaged and collaborative future.

How We're Making a Difference

Through debate education, students learn that seeing all sides of an argument isn't about abandoning their beliefs‚Äîit's about strengthening their ability to engage in meaningful dialogue. When middle schoolers practice arguing both for and against a position, they develop empathy, critical thinking, and the communication skills necessary to bridge divides rather than deepen them.

This isn't just about winning competitions. It's about creating a generation of citizens who can listen, understand, collaborate, and find common ground even when they disagree. By starting this education in middle school, we're shaping future leaders who will approach civic engagement with nuance, respect, and open-mindedness.

Our Programs

Perspective-Building Workshops

Interactive sessions where students practice arguing multiple sides of real-world issues, learning that understanding different viewpoints strengthens rather than weakens their own reasoning.

Collaborative Debate Rounds

Hands-on debate experience where students work together to explore complex topics, receiving constructive feedback in a supportive environment that values listening as much as speaking.

Mentorship Program

One-on-one pairing with Eastlake student mentors who model respectful discourse, critical analysis, and how to engage with difficult topics while maintaining empathy and open-mindedness.

Skills for Life & Democracy

  • Multiperspective Thinking: Analyze complex issues from all angles.
  • Empathetic Communication: Understand and articulate viewpoints different from your own.
  • Critical Analysis: Identify credible information and strong arguments.
  • Confident Expression: Build public speaking skills for school and life.
  • Collaborative Spirit: Practice respectful discourse and mutual learning.
  • Civic Readiness: Engage thoughtfully with important issues.
